**Detailed Breakdown of the Study Guide Questions**

**Lesson 1: Immigration**

1.  **Where did most immigrants come from before and after 1880/1890?**
    *   *Before:* Primarily Northern and Western Europe (e.g., Great Britain, Ireland, Germany, Scandinavia).
    *   *After:* Increasingly Southern and Eastern Europe (e.g., Italy, Russia, Poland, Austria-Hungary).
2.  **What were the push/pull factors for immigrants?**
    *   *Push Factors:* Reasons people left their home countries (e.g., poverty, famine, religious persecution, political unrest, lack of jobs, war).
    *   *Pull Factors:* Reasons people were attracted to the United States (e.g., economic opportunities, religious freedom, political stability, available land, jobs).
3.  **What was the immigration processing station in NY and San Francisco called?**
    *   *New York:* Ellis Island
    *   *San Francisco:* Angel Island
4.  **Describe what happens during immigrant processing (bit.ly/AngelEllisIslands)?**
    *   *Ellis Island:* Medical inspections, legal inspections, questions about background, purpose for coming to America, etc.  Focus on quick processing of large numbers.
    *   *Angel Island:* More rigorous and lengthy interrogations, often detained for extended periods.  Targeted primarily at Asian immigrants.
5.  **Define Nativism**
    *   The policy of protecting the interests of native-born or established inhabitants against those of immigrants. Includes prejudice against foreign-born people.
6.  **Define the Chinese Exclusion Act**
    *   A law passed in 1882 that prohibited Chinese laborers from entering the United States. It was a significant example of nativism and discrimination.
7.  **Describe the new technology in urban areas?**
    *   Skyscrapers (steel frame construction), electric trolleys, subways, electric lighting, indoor plumbing, telephones.
8.  **What caused urban growth?**
    *   Immigration, migration from rural areas (seeking jobs), industrialization (factories located in cities).
9.  **What was yellow journalism and which two journalists used it the most?**
    *   Yellow journalism: Exaggerated and sensationalized news reporting, often used to sell newspapers and influence public opinion.
    *   Key journalists: William Randolph Hearst and Joseph Pulitzer.



**Lesson 2: Progressive Era and Reformers**

*   **PROGRESSIVE ERA:** Started ?_________ Ended? WWI

    *   *Started:* Roughly the 1890s.
*   **Who were the Progressive Presidents?**
    *   Theodore Roosevelt, William Howard Taft, and Woodrow Wilson.
*   **What is a muckraker (bit.ly/MuckrakerS ) and role of these REFORMERS?**
    *   Muckraker: Investigative journalists who exposed corruption and social problems.
    *   Role: To raise public awareness of issues and inspire reform.
*   **What were the amendments to the Constitution passed during the Progressive era?**
    *   16th Amendment: Income tax.
    *   17th Amendment: Direct election of senators.
    *   18th Amendment: Prohibition of alcohol.
    *   19th Amendment: Women's suffrage (right to vote).
*   **What is the Sherman AntiTrust Act and the Clayton AntiTrust Act? Which Presidents used them?**
    *   Sherman Antitrust Act: Prohibited monopolies and restraint of trade.
    *   Clayton Antitrust Act: Strengthened the Sherman Act, outlawed specific practices that hurt competition.
    *   Presidents: Theodore Roosevelt was a major user, Taft, and Wilson.
*   **What laws were passed to protect consumers?**
    *   Pure Food and Drug Act: Required accurate labeling of food and drugs.
    *   Meat Inspection Act: Established federal inspection of meatpacking plants.
*   **What three ‘C” reforms were the focus of Teddy Roosevelt’s “Square Deal”?**
    *   Conservation of natural resources, Control of corporations, and Consumer protection.
*   **How was the country/role of government different AFTER Teddy Roosevelt became POTUS?**
    *   The government became more active in regulating the economy and protecting the public interest.  Increased federal power, especially executive power.
